Speedy was a 313 ton whaler and convict ship that was dispatched in 1799 from England to Australia.

Speedy conducted whaling expeditions in the Pacific Ocean in 1793 and transported stores in 1794 to Port Jackson under the command of Thomas Melville. Under the command of George Quested, she sailed from England on 24 November 1799, with 53 female convicts. She arrived at Port Jackson on 15 April 1800. Three convicts died on the voyage. The Speedy left Port Jackson in July 1800 for whaling in the South Seas.
